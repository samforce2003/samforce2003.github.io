## 1. Mathematical Preliminaries

### 1.1 From Four Axioms to Market Structure

**Axiom 1 (Structure as Foundation)**. Every market is a structure $S = (\mathcal{X}, \mathcal{R})$, where $\mathcal{X}$ is the set of market participants and assets, and $\mathcal{R}$ is the set of trading relations (buy/sell/hold/arbitrage).

**Axiom 2 (Difference Creates Being)**. The market exists because $\Delta(\text{buy}, \text{sell}) \neq 0$ вЂ” perfect symmetry would annihilate all trades.

**Axiom 3 (Coupling Creates Novelty)**. When market A and market B interact via cross-border capital flows, their coupling $S_A \bowtie S_B$ generates pricing structures irreducible to either market alone.

**Axiom 4 (Self-Reference Has Limits)**. A market cannot perfectly price itself. External observation (cross-market arbitrage) is required for price discovery.

### 1.2 The Global Gauge Field О›\*

**Definition 1.1 (Global Pricing Gauge Field).** Let $\mathcal{M}$ be the set of all financial markets. The *global pricing gauge field* $\Lambda^*$ is a principal $G$-bundle:

$$\pi: P \to \mathcal{M}$$

where the structure group $G = U(1) \times \text{Diff}(\mathbb{R}^+) $ encodes (a) the global phase of capital flows ($U(1)$) and (b) the scale transformations of asset prices ($\text{Diff}(\mathbb{R}^+)$). The base manifold $\mathcal{M}$ is metrized by the *coupling distance*:

$$d(S_s, S_t) = \inf_{\gamma} \int_\gamma \sqrt{g_{ab} dx^a dx^b}$$

where $g_{ab}$ is the Bures metric on the space of market density operators, pulled back from the universal configuration space via Axiom 1.

**Definition 1.2 (Fiber over a Market).** For each market $S \in \mathcal{M}$, the fiber $\pi^{-1}(S) \cong G$ represents the set of *allowable price gauges* вЂ” equivalent representations of the same underlying asset values under different currency denominations and numГ©raire choices.

**Definition 1.3 (Connection).** The gauge field $\Lambda^*$ is equipped with a connection 1-form $\omega \in \Omega^1(P, \mathfrak{g})$, where $\mathfrak{g} = \mathfrak{u}(1) \oplus \text{Vect}(\mathbb{R}^+)$ is the Lie algebra of $G$. The connection defines *parallel transport*: given a structural event $E$ at market $S_s$ and a path $\gamma: [0,1] \to \mathcal{M}$ from $S_s$ to $S_t$, the transported event is:

$$\mathcal{T}_\gamma(E) = \text{Hol}_\gamma(\omega) \cdot E$$

where $\text{Hol}_\gamma(\omega) \in G$ is the holonomy of $\omega$ along $\gamma$.

### 1.3 Structural Events as Sections

**Definition 1.4 (Structural Event).** A *structural event* $E$ on market $S$ is a section of the associated vector bundle $E = P \times_G V$, where $V$ is the 9-dimensional vector space of structural fingerprints (coupling gradient spectrum, decoherence rate, О› anchor phase, coupling participation degree). ## 2. The Coupling Bridge Theorem

### 2.1 Migration Operator

**Definition 2.1 (Migration Operator).** The *migration operator* $\mathcal{M}$ maps a structural event $E_s$ from source market $S_s$ to target market $S_t$, with time delay $\tau$:

$$\mathcal{M}(E_s, S_t, \tau) = \Pi_{S_t} \circ \mathcal{T}_{\gamma_{\min}}(E_s)$$

where:
- $\gamma_{\min}$ is the geodesic on $\mathcal{M}$ connecting $S_s$ to $S_t$ (minimal coupling distance path)
- $\mathcal{T}_{\gamma_{\min}}$ is parallel transport along $\gamma_{\min}$ with respect to the connection $\omega$
- $\Pi_{S_t}: V \to \mathbb{R}^9_{\text{obs}}$ is the projection onto the *observable subspace* at $S_t$
- $\tau = \text{length}(\gamma_{\min}) / v_c$ is the propagation delay, where $v_c$ is the characteristic coupling propagation speed

### 2.2 Nested Structure of Markets

**Definition 2.2 (Structural Nesting Depth).** Every market $S$ admits a filtration by structural layers:

$$S^{(0)} \subset S^{(1)} \subset \cdots \subset S^{(k)} = S$$

where $S^{(j)}$ incorporates constraints up to layer $j$. The *nesting depth* $N(S)$ is the maximal $k$ such that $S^{(k-1)} \neq S^{(k)}$.

**Definition 2.3 (Layer Difference).** The *layer difference* $\Delta N(S_s, S_t)$ between source and target markets is:

$$\Delta N = \| \mathbf{N}(S_s) - \mathbf{N}(S_t) \|_{\ell^2}$$

where $\mathbf{N}(S) = (N_{\text{macro}}, N_{\text{meso}}, N_{\text{micro}})$ is the nesting vector across three structural scales.

### 2.3 The Fidelity Formula

**Theorem 2.1 (Coupling Bridge Theorem вЂ” Fidelity).** The fidelity $F$ with which a structural event $E_s$ migrates from source market $S_s$ to target market $S_t$ is:

$$F(E_s \to S_t) = R(\Delta N) \cdot P(\Delta\theta) \cdot (1 - \varepsilon_{\text{obs}})$$

where:

**(a) Nesting Rate Decay:**

$$R(\Delta N) = \frac{1}{1 + |\Delta N|}$$

*Proof.* By Axioms 2-3, each structural layer acts as an independent constraint on the transported event. The fidelity through $k$ independent constraint layers follows the multiplicative accumulation of constraint satisfaction probabilities. When constraints are approximately independent, the joint fidelity is $\prod_{j=1}^{|\Delta N|} (1 - c_j)$. For small uniform constraint strength $c_j \approx c$, this gives $(1-c)^{|\Delta N|} \approx e^{-c|\Delta N|}$. Expanding to first order: $1/(1 + c|\Delta N|)$. Setting $c=1$ (natural units) yields $R(\Delta N) = 1/(1 + |\Delta N|)$. в€Ћ

**(b) О› Phase Locking:**

$$P(\Delta\theta) = \cos^2(\Delta\theta)$$

where $\Delta\theta$ is the phase difference between the О› asymmetry anchors of source and target markets.

*Proof.* The global gauge field $\Lambda^*$ has a $U(1)$ component whose holonomy phase is $\theta_\Lambda$. Two markets are in *О› phase resonance* when their gauge phases are aligned. The amplitude of a transported mode $\psi_m$ upon projection onto a target with phase offset $\Delta\theta$ is the inner product $\langle \psi_m | e^{i\Delta\theta} \psi_m \rangle = e^{i\Delta\theta} \|\psi_m\|^2$. The observable intensity (squared amplitude) is $|e^{i\Delta\theta}|^2 \cdot \|\psi_m\|^4 = \|\psi_m\|^4$, but only the *in-phase component* $|\langle \psi_m | \Pi_{\text{in-phase}} | \psi_m \rangle|^2$ is transmitted вЂ” amplitude orthogonal to the target phase is reflected. The in-phase projection amplitude is $\cos(\Delta\theta)$, giving transmitted intensity $\cos^2(\Delta\theta)$. в€Ћ

**(c) Self-Reference Observation Noise:**

$$\varepsilon_{\text{obs}} = \frac{\dim(\ker \Pi_{S_t})}{\dim(V)}$$

where $\ker \Pi_{S_t}$ is the unobservable subspace at target market $S_t$ вЂ” the structural blind spot that cannot be eliminated (Axiom 4 direct corollary).

*Proof.* By Axiom 4, the target market $S_t$ has an irreducible blind spot in its self-observation. The projection $\Pi_{S_t}$ onto the observable subspace has kernel dimension $\dim(\ker \Pi_{S_t}) \geq 1$. The fraction of the structural fingerprint that falls into this blind spot is $\dim(\ker \Pi_{S_t}) / \dim(V)$. Since the fingerprint components are in general position with respect to the blind spot, the expected information loss is exactly this dimensional ratio. в€Ћ

### 2.4 Necessary and Sufficient Conditions

**Theorem 2.2 (Migration Conditions).** A structural event $E_s$ successfully migrates from $S_s$ to $S_t$ ($F > 0$) if and only if:

1. **Triadic Completeness:** $\text{rank}(\Lambda_t) \geq 3$

*Proof.*

**(2) Layer threshold.** From the fidelity formula, $F \propto 1/(1+|\Delta N|)$. As $|\Delta N| \to \infty$, $F \to 0$. **(4) Phase resonance.** From $P(\Delta\theta) = \cos^2(\Delta\theta)$, we have $P(\pi/4) = 1/2$. ## 3. Migration as Parallel Transport on a Fiber Bundle

### 3.1 The Co-Mode Coupling Perturbation $\psi_m$

**Definition 3.1 (Co-Mode).** A structural event $E_s$ at market $S_s$ excites a *co-mode perturbation* $\psi_m$ in the global gauge field $\Lambda^*$. Formally, $\psi_m$ is a section of the adjoint bundle $\text{Ad}(P) = P \times_G \mathfrak{g}$ satisfying the linearized field equation:

$$\Delta_\Lambda \psi_m + m^2 \psi_m = \delta_{S_s} \otimes \mathbf{F}(E_s)$$

where $\Delta_\Lambda = d_\omega^* d_\omega$ is the gauge-covariant Laplacian, $m$ is the mass gap of the co-mode (inverse of the characteristic propagation length), and $\delta_{S_s} \otimes \mathbf{F}(E_s)$ is the source term localized at $S_s$ with amplitude equal to the structural fingerprint.

**Proposition 3.1 (Propagation of $\psi_m$).** The co-mode $\psi_m$ propagates along geodesics of $\mathcal{M}$ with attenuation factor $e^{-m \cdot d(S_s, S_t)}$, where $d$ is the coupling distance. The parallel transport of $\mathbf{F}(E_s)$ along $\gamma_{\min}$ is the evaluation of $\psi_m$ at $S_t$:

$$\mathcal{T}_{\gamma_{\min}}(\mathbf{F}(E_s)) = \psi_m(S_t) = e^{-m \cdot d(S_s, S_t)} \cdot \text{Hol}_{\gamma_{\min}}(\omega) \cdot \mathbf{F}(E_s)$$

*Proof.* The field equation $\Delta_\Lambda \psi_m + m^2 \psi_m = \delta_{S_s} \otimes \mathbf{F}(E_s)$ is the Klein-Gordon equation on the curved manifold $(\mathcal{M}, g_{ab})$ with a point source. On a Riemannian manifold, the Green's function for the massive Klein-Gordon operator decays as $G(d) \propto d^{-(n-2)/2} K_{(n-2)/2}(m d)$ in $n$ dimensions, where $K_\nu$ is the modified Bessel function. For $n > 2$, this decays exponentially as $e^{-md}$ at large distances. Incorporating the gauge holonomy gives the stated result. в€Ћ

### 3.2 The Wilson Loop Condition

**Theorem 3.1 (Wilson Loop Criterion).** The migration fidelity $F(E_s \to S_t) > 0$ if and only if the Wilson loop $W(\gamma)$ around the closed path $\gamma = \gamma_{\min}(S_s \to S_t) \circ \gamma_{\text{return}}$ is non-trivial:

$$W(\gamma) = \text{Tr} \left( \mathcal{P} \exp\left( \oint_\gamma \omega \right) \right) \neq 1$$

where $\mathcal{P}$ denotes path ordering.

*Proof.* The parallel transport of $\mathbf{F}(E_s)$ along $\gamma_{\min}$ yields a non-zero result at $S_t$ iff the holonomy $\text{Hol}_{\gamma_{\min}}(\omega)$ is non-trivial. The Wilson loop around the return path measures exactly this holonomy. $W(\gamma) = 1$ implies trivial holonomy, meaning the gauge field is flat between $S_s$ and $S_t$, and no structural information is transported. в€Ћ

## 4. Self-Referential Collapse of the Migration Operator

**Theorem 4.1 (Self-Referential Collapse).** When market participants learn the migration pattern $\mathcal{M}$ and begin to trade on it, the migration channel undergoes self-referential collapse:

$$\lim_{t \to \infty} F_t(E_s \to S_t) = 0$$

where $F_t$ is the fidelity at time $t$ under active exploitation of $\mathcal{M}$.

*Proof.* Let $\mathcal{M}_t$ be the migration operator as perceived by market participants at time $t$. The act of trading on $\mathcal{M}_t$ alters the coupling between $S_s$ and $S_t$, thereby changing the connection $\omega$ on $\Lambda^*$:

$$\omega \to \omega + \delta\omega(\mathcal{M}_t)$$

where $\delta\omega(\mathcal{M}_t)$ is the gauge perturbation induced by arbitrage flows. This perturbation compresses the layer difference: $\Delta N_t = \Delta N_0 \cdot e^{-\alpha t}$ where $\alpha$ is the arbitrage intensity. Simultaneously, the target market's self-referential blind spot expands: $\varepsilon_{\text{obs}}(t) = \varepsilon_{\text{obs}}(0) + \beta(1 - e^{-\gamma t})$, because participants' models of $\mathcal{M}$ add new self-referential nodes that increase the unobservable subspace dimension.

As $t \to \infty$, two things happen:
1. $R(\Delta N_t) = 1/(1 + |\Delta N_0|e^{-\alpha t}) \to 1$ (the markets appear structurally closer)
2. $\varepsilon_{\text{obs}}(t) \to \varepsilon_{\text{obs}}(0) + \beta$ (the blind spot expands)

The net fidelity $F_t = R(\Delta N_t) \cdot P(\Delta\theta) \cdot (1 - \varepsilon_{\text{obs}}(t))$ becomes:

$$\lim_{t \to \infty} F_t = 1 \cdot P(\Delta\theta) \cdot (1 - \varepsilon_{\text{obs}}(0) - \beta)$$

If $\beta > 1 - \varepsilon_{\text{obs}}(0)$ вЂ” i.e., the arbitrage-induced blind spot exceeds the original observable fraction вЂ” then $F_t$ crosses zero and becomes negative (meaning: the migration pattern *inverts*, producing anti-correlated predictions, which is worse than random).
