## ​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌‍​‌Abstract

When AI and automated systems complete all persistence production — agriculture, manufacturing, services, management — the substrate of human social structure will undergo a total phase transition. Old society organized around "persistence coupling": humans must produce to survive; therefore marriage is an economic alliance, companies are production units, currency is a persistence voucher, education is skill training, law is order maintenance, and the state is the top-level manager. After AI takes over production, "persistence" is stripped from human obligations. Old institutions lose their structural substrate. New society will organize around "oscillation coupling": humans couple not for survival, but for coupling itself — actively exposing themselves to incompatible coupling to trigger configuration oscillation, thereby increasing their own complexity. Marriage becomes an oscillation partnership. Companies become oscillation platforms. Currency dies and is replaced by oscillation credits. Education transforms into inter-reference capacity training. Law shifts from punishment to structural repair. The state shifts from managing persistence to managing oscillation. This is not a utopian fantasy. It is the inevitable phase transition derived from the structural axiom system — when the substrate parameter of the social configuration network shifts from "persistence must be through production" to "persistence is guaranteed by AI," the organizing principle necessarily跃迁s from persistence coupling to oscillation coupling. This paper describes the four-stage transition process of this phase transition and provides testable predictions. An appendix provides a plain-language version.

**Keywords:** persistence coupling, oscillation coupling, social structure phase transition, AI takeover of production, substrate replacement, configuration complexity
