    **2. New Interpretation from the Structural Perspective**


    Decoherence is not a probabilistic event — it is deterministic constraint convergence:


    1. Quantum superposition state = coexistence of multiple relation topologies. At this point, the system has not been subjected to global constraints.

    2. The quantum system and environment (two independent structures) undergo coupling. Global constraints are introduced, forming the **Joint Phase Constraint Space**.

    3. Within this space, only specific **stable fixed points** exist. The manifestation of the final state is deterministic convergence after constraint resolution, not random selection.
